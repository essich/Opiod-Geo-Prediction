Opioid Geo-Predictor

Ohio has the fifth highest rate of drug overdose deaths (24.6 deaths per 100,000) in the United States.

Unintentional drug overdose has become the leading casue of injury-related deaths in Ohio.

The state has cracked down and implemented internal controls to reduce the supply of opioids such as limiting prescriptions and forming stricter guidelines to receive them. However, as overdose realted deaths begin to slightly decrease since the big outbreak in 2010, opioids remain to be a prevalent issue in the state of Ohio.

Our team has developed a model to help predict potential counties at risk of opioid deaths in Ohio 2020.
